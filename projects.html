Analyzing Nullified Interceptions in the NFL: A Case Study on Patrick Mahomes

From 2018 to 2023, I analyzed nullified interceptions in the NFL, focusing on Patrick Mahomes to determine if he had an unusually high number compared to other quarterbacks. Using R (nflfastR, tidyverse), I pulled and cleaned play-by-play data, identifying interceptions negated by penalties and filling in missing QB names based on game starters.

After exporting the dataset to Python (pandas, scipy, matplotlib), I conducted statistical tests, including a Z-score analysis, Mann-Whitney U Test, and bootstrap resampling. The results indicate that Mahomes' 19 nullified interceptions are a strong statistical outlier, with a Z-score of 4.80, far exceeding other quarterbacks. These findings suggest that Mahomes has had significantly more interceptions nullified by penalties than expected, warranting further investigation.

Madden Football Defensive Analysis App

I created this app in early 2024, driven by my love for football and Madden. The goal was to improve my in-game performance by building a tool that could help me analyze defensive plays. The app uses a decision tree algorithm that asks users a series of questions to identify the type of defensive formation.

Once identified, it suggests offensive plays that exploit the defense's weaknesses. This project was my first experience using Swift (and possibly my last, haha), and it was a great opportunity to dive into app development while merging my passion for football strategy.
